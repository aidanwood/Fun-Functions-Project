Why Is This Function Fun?

The Bezier graph is fun because it does not take the usual coordinates of a function, x and y. This is because it only continues for a certain distance and does not go on forever like a line or quadratic. It takes a variable called t, where t <= 1 and t >= 0. Each value of t will give you a different coordinate, so the more values of t you put in the more accurate your graph will be. For my graph, I calculated t with 1000 different numbers, but a longer graph needs more different intervals than a smaller one. In addition to varying values of t in your X equation and Y equation, you need to plug in the parameters. (x0, y0) is the first point, (x3, y3) is the end point, and both (x2, y2) and (x1, y1) are control, points. The curve goes through the first point and the end point, and the curve is adjusted using the control points. Bezier curves are used in computer graphics because they are very easy to construct, and with enough control points can form any continuous shape. They are used in most side scrolled games, most notably Happy Wheels.
